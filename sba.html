What is an SBA Loan?

An SBA Loan is a small business loan partially guaranteed by the government (Small Business Administration). Because the government backs a significant portion of the loan, it reduces the risk for lenders. This allows small businesses to access capital with highly favorable terms, lower down payments, and longer repayment periods than traditional bank loans.

How it works:

  • You apply through an SBA-approved lender (like a bank or credit union), not directly through the government.
  • The SBA guarantees up to 85% of the loan amount, protecting the lender in case of default.
  • If approved, funds can be used for working capital, equipment, real estate, or debt refinancing.

Key Benefits:

  • Lower Down Payments – Keep more of your cash on hand for daily operations.
  • Longer Repayment Terms – Up to 10 years for working capital and up to 25 years for real estate, keeping monthly payments low.
  • Competitive Interest Rates – SBA loans offer some of the lowest interest rates available in the commercial lending market.

Qualifying Factors:

  • Must be a for-profit business operating within the required region.
  • Strong personal and business credit scores.
  • Solid financial history with proof of steady revenue and profitability.

Which Businesses Are Ideal for This Type of Financing?

SBA Loans are perfect for established businesses looking for substantial funding to expand, purchase commercial real estate, acquire another business, or refinance existing high-interest debt. They are ideal for business owners who have a strong credit profile and can afford to wait through a slightly longer approval process.

Ideal for Businesses In Industries Such As:

  • Established Small Businesses & Franchises
  • Medical, Dental & Veterinary Practices
  • Manufacturing & Industrial Companies
  • Retailers planning major expansions
  • Businesses purchasing Commercial Real Estate

Benefits:

  • Maximize daily cash flow due to extended repayment terms and low monthly payments.
  • Access to large funding amounts (often up to $5 million).
  • Flexible use of funds across almost any legitimate business need.
